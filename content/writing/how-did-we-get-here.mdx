In Minecraft there's an advancement called *How Did We Get Here?*. You get it by having every status effect on you at the same time — which basically never happens on purpose. Good title for this story, because none of it was planned either.

I was born in May 2000, so the years below line up with my age pretty well.

## 2009 · It starts with Legos

When I was 9, my parents gave me my first Lego Star Wars set for Christmas, not knowing they were sending me down the Lego rabbit hole. I built everything I could get my hands on. I even made Lego YouTube videos — they're still on YouTube somewhere — and I was editing the videos myself, at 9!

## 2011 · A world within the world

Around 11, I hit a problem: I couldn't afford the Legos I wanted. So I started looking for a software that would let me build Legos online, where pieces are free. I never found it. But I found a game that would shape the rest of my childhood: Minecraft.

Minecraft is a real world within the world to express creativity. I started on Minecraft Classic — limited map size, and no way to save. So I kept my computer on to not lose my progress. Just left it running, days at a time.

Then I wanted more, and I started looking at the game files. I remember staring at the `.jar` and `.class` files like they were crazy magic characters shaping the game. I found images inside, edited them, and saw it was actually changing them in the game. I had discovered texture packs before knowing texture packs existed (haha).

## 2012 · One line in Blocks.java

Texture packs were not giving enough room for creativity either. I joined Ironcraft, a French coding forum, and started decompiling the game. At this point I still had no idea I was starting to code.

Then came the déclic. In a file called `Blocks.java`, I copy-pasted a line, changed it a bit — and it actually added a block in the game.

This felt crazy. I could actually see the effect of what I wrote, standing there in the world. I was 12, and I was coding without ever having decided to "learn programming".

## 2013 · Expencial

My first server. Very little — but I made my first 72 euros selling ranks with Starpass, and at 13 that felt huge. My stuff, running for other people, and they paid for it.

## 2014–2016 · HardFight

Then I worked for HardFight, a massive French modded server — more than 150,000 registered players. Real users at real scale, while I was still in collège.

## 2016 · Cactucea

A Discord alternative that never came to life. But that's where I learnt sockets and touched actual web tech for the first time (remember, I come from Java!).

## 2017–2018 · ProClient

At 17 I built ProClient, a modded version of Minecraft made to improve the player experience on Funcraft.net. It passed 250,000 downloads.

## 2018 · Scraft

A modern OpenGL 3D engine, with raytracing. Pure early-dev "I want to do everything" ego, but I learnt a lot about low level there.

## Same thing ever since

Looking back, it's always been the same move. Creativity hits a wall → I go after the tool. Legos too expensive → look for a software. The software doesn't exist → Minecraft. The game too limited → open its files. The files are compiled → decompile them.

Everything since — freelance, ClinicPilot, Cial — is the same move, just bigger. Cial is literally a workspace that opens its own files and changes itself. So in a way, I ended up building the software I was looking for at 11. The blocks are just code now.
